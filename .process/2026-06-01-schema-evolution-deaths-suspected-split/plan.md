# Plan, schema evolution: bifurcate deaths and refine suspected

**Change-id:** 2026-06-01-schema-evolution-deaths-suspected-split
**Classification:** Critical (publisher-of-record schema change; ripples through ingestion, reconciler, contract, public exports, brief render, narrative docs, website mirror).
**Branch:** `feat/2026-06-01-schema-evolution-deaths-split` (off `d59fdee`, the canonical-pipeline rehydration of `4989113`).

## Intent

INRB published two new SitReps (`#015` on May 29 and `#016` on May 30) that surface fields the upstream source has tracked all along but the LOVS schema previously collapsed:

1. **Deaths now declared as two separate series**: `cumul deces parmi confirmes` (clinically confirmed) vs `cumul deces suspects` (under investigation). Our schema previously emitted a single `deaths` ReconciledCount whose primary was a composition of confirmed + suspected, yielding a misleading `247` headline that read as clinically confirmed when it was 17 confirmed + 230 suspected.
2. **Suspected cases now split into active vs cumulative**: SitRep `#016` introduces `cas suspects en cours d'investigation` (220) + `cas suspects en isolement` (101) as the active stock, while the cumulative series (`cumul cas suspects`) continues separately.
3. **Confirmed cases now also split between cumulative and active**: SitRep `#016` adds `cas confirmes actifs` (= cumul - deaths - cured = 238 for May 30) as a derived headline.

This change adopts the INRB schema verbatim. The single-`deaths` field is retired in favor of `deaths_confirmed` and `deaths_suspected`. The single-`suspected` field is split into `suspected_active` and `suspected_cumulative`. The carried-forward reason enum is rebuilt to reflect operational reality: the upstream source is not pausing or stopping declaration, they are iterating on their schema mid-emerging-outbreak and we hold the right-to-flexibility framing as an operating condition (not a public narrative).

## Success Criteria

Five binary checks:

1. `lovs.lovs_reconciler.CARRIED_FORWARD_REASONS == frozenset({"source_schema_evolved", "awaiting_next_publication"})` and the mirror in `lovs.snapshot_contract` is byte-identical.
2. `OutbreakSnapshot.reported_deaths` is a `dict[str, ReconciledCount]` keyed by `"confirmed"` and `"suspected"`; no consumer references a single-class `reported_deaths` attribute.
3. `OutbreakSnapshot.reported_counts` carries `"suspected_active"` and `"suspected_cumulative"` keys whenever the upstream source supplies the split (back-compat: legacy single `"suspected"` rows are accepted by ingestion but normalized to `"suspected_cumulative"` at the boundary).
4. The May 29, May 30, and May 31 snapshots build deterministically with `python3 release_snapshot.py --as-of YYYY-MM-DD --check` passing; May 29 and May 30 use the new SitRep numbers with no LOCF on fields the SitReps declare; May 31 uses full LOCF from May 30 with reason `awaiting_next_publication`.
5. The brief renders both split series inline (no new sections) and surfaces an LOCF provenance footnote whenever any headline field is carried forward.

## Non-Goals

- Do **not** wire the orphan `svgs["per_zone_snapshot"]` SVG slot generated by `make_brief.py` but never embedded in `brief.html`; that slot is unrelated to the schema split and would balloon scope.
- Do **not** rewrite or rebase the `data/calibration-ledger.json` immutable pre-commitment record. The 19 June 2026 calibration targets corridor risk, not death counts; the field rename is non-load-bearing for ledger resolution.
- Do **not** retroactively re-publish the May 28 snapshot. The shipped artefact stands; the May 29 + 30 + 31 builds are forward-only.

## Constraints

- **Byte-determinism of `snapshot_content_seed`** must hold: the new `reported_deaths` dict must serialize in sorted key order before hashing.
- **Back-compat with older snapshot consumers**: the public schema bumps version (`schemas/public_snapshot.schema.json`) but the reader for the website mirror must tolerate snapshots from both pre- and post-split families during the rollout window. Document the bump in `CHANGELOG.md`.
- **Snapshot contract** validator must accept the new field set and reject the deprecated `"source_stopped_declaring"` and `"source_changed_methodology"` reason codes.
- **Brief render** must fall back to the single-range headline form when split fields are absent (defensive against partial data), and never display a confirmed/suspected count as if it were the other.

## Approach

1. **Enum migration**: replace `CARRIED_FORWARD_REASONS` in both `lovs/lovs_reconciler.py` and `lovs/snapshot_contract.py`; update validators; update CLI `--carried-forward-reason` defaults in `refresh_pipeline.py`.
2. **Reconciler split**: change `OutbreakSnapshot.reported_deaths` to `dict[str, ReconciledCount]`; update `_reconcile_count` call sites to ingest `deaths_confirmed` and `deaths_suspected` from normalized content; add `suspected_active` and `suspected_cumulative` to the `_CASE_CLASSES` iteration; update `_build_conflict_notes`, `_deaths_to_confirmed_tension`, and `snapshot_content_seed` to walk the new shape with sorted keys.
3. **Contract + schema update**: bump `schemas/public_snapshot.schema.json` to require the new field set; update `data/snapshot_contract.json`; widen `lovs/public_exports.py` allowlist; update `lovs/website_bundle_parity.py` `COUNT_METRICS`.
4. **Ingestion**: rewrite the deaths reconciliation block in `refresh_pipeline.py` to drop the cross-class 247 composition rule. Add SitRep `#015` and `#016` source entries to `data/bundibugyo-2026/manifest.json` with raw bytes captured under `data/bundibugyo-2026/raw/`. Update `source_ingest.py` DRC MoH dashboard routing to send `deces` to `deaths_suspected` (cumulative bulletin counts are unconfirmed clinical totals).
5. **Render + narrative**: update `make_brief.py` headline template for both splits and add an LOCF provenance footnote using a new `readable_locf_reason(code)` helper. Rewrite `README.md`, `NUMBERS_AUDIT.md`, and `WORKED_SNAPSHOT_REVIEW.md` to remove the "INRB stopped declaring" framing and adopt the neutral "schema evolved / awaiting next publication" vocabulary. Update test fixtures across `tests/` and add three new test files (`test_schema_split_deaths.py`, `test_schema_split_suspected_active_cumulative.py`, `test_locf_reason_enum.py`).

## Risks

- **Hash drift**: the `snapshot_content_seed` change will produce a different hash for any back-compat re-run of the May 28 snapshot. This is intentional and unavoidable; the May 28 artefact stays as-shipped, and the byte-determinism contract resumes from the May 29 build forward.
- **Consumer churn**: any code path that read `snapshot.reported_deaths.primary_value` will break. The migration is a hard rename, not a deprecation alias, because preserving the legacy shape would let stale consumers display a misleading headline. Full sweep of consumers is in `assumptions.md`.
- **CFR-tension semantics**: `_deaths_to_confirmed_tension` now compares confirmed cases to `deaths_confirmed` only (not summed deaths). This raises the tension threshold (deaths/confirmed is closer to the real CFR), which is the intended outcome. Document the change in `NUMBERS_AUDIT.md`.
- **Schema-version interaction with the website mirror**: the website snapshot reader must tolerate both shapes during the rollout. Solution: gate the reader on a `schema_version` discriminant and route both shapes through a normalizer before render.

## Skip Decision

None. This is a Critical change to the upstream-data contract and must run the full 9-phase gate sequence (plan, glossary, assumptions, implementation, review, polish, stress, red team, stage).
